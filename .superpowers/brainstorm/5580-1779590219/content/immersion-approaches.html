How immersive do we go?

Three approaches — from surgical to full transformation. All preserve the 5-step demo flow.

A

Notification storm only (Step 5)

Keep the flow as-is. At Step 5, replace the single Halo nudge on the phone with a cascade of 5–6 stacked notifications from real apps — iMessage from roommate, Slack from coworker, Instagram from friends at the pub, Google Calendar missed deadline. They pile up staggered, then Halo cuts through them all in mint. Judges feel the chaos without touching the narrative copy.

Cost: Low — phone shell only. Wow: High at Step 5.

B

Second-person copy + notification storm (Recommended)

Two changes: (1) All narrative copy flips to "you" — "You have 9 unplanned hours", "Your research paper: 0 hours in 12 days", "Before you say yes" (already there). (2) Phone at Step 5 shows the notification storm. Judges aren't watching Alex — they ARE Alex, and then they feel the interruption storm hit their own phone. The drift message lands as a mirror, not a report.

Cost: Medium — copy edits across all steps + phone storm. Wow: Very high — emotional throughout.

C

Progressive notification build across all steps

The phone is visible from Step 1 (small, in corner). Each step adds a notification to it — roommate texts in Step 1, coworker Slack in Step 2, pub invite in Step 3. By Step 5 the phone is buried in chaos and Halo fires. Judges watch the accumulation happen in real time as the demo progresses. Most cinematic — also highest engineering cost and highest demo-fail risk.

Cost: High — persistent phone across all steps, state management. Wow: Highest — but fragile.
